# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-03-15*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Fellowship-trained urogynecologist providing expert pelvic floor care in Green Bay, WI
**What it does:** ryanstewart.com is the personal educational website for Dr. Ryan Stewart, a fellowship-trained urogynecologist. It serves as a patient education resource and acquisition channel — separate from his employer's website.
**Product category:** Specialty medical practice (urogynecology / FPMRS)
**Product type:** Healthcare service / personal physician website
**Business model:** Employed physician at a hospital-based practice; fee-for-service via insurance reimbursement; 50+ accepted plans

## Target Audience
**Target patients:** Women in NE Wisconsin and Michigan's Upper Peninsula experiencing pelvic floor disorders
**Decision-makers:** The patients themselves (women, typically 35-75+), sometimes influenced by referring PCPs or OB/GYNs
**Primary use case:** Finding a subspecialist who can diagnose and treat their pelvic condition
**Jobs to be done:**
- Understand what's happening with their body
- Find a qualified specialist nearby
- Get effective treatment that fits their life and goals

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Pelvic floor disorders significantly impact quality of life but patients often suffer in silence — embarrassment, not knowing specialists exist, or thinking it's "just part of aging"
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- PCPs and general OB/GYNs lack subspecialty training
- Urologists treat some overlap conditions but aren't trained in the full scope
- Patients may not know urogynecology exists as a distinct subspecialty
**What it costs them:** Daily discomfort, social isolation, activity limitations, emotional distress
**Emotional tension:** Embarrassment about symptoms, fear of surgery, uncertainty about whether treatment can help

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:**
- **Dr. Yoon Chun, MD** (Emplify Health, Green Bay) — Urogynecologist, Wayne State trained, 4.9/5 stars (265 ratings). Experienced but no fellowship listed on profile. Korean-speaking. Same metro area.
- **Dr. Colin Johnson, MD** (Women's Care of Wisconsin, Appleton) — Urogynecologist, fellowship-trained at University of Iowa. Patient education focus similar to Dr. Stewart. ~30 min south of Green Bay.

**Secondary:** General OB/GYNs and urologists who treat some pelvic conditions without fellowship training

**Indirect:** Doing nothing / living with symptoms; OTC products (pads, devices)

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- Fellowship-trained in urogynecology & reconstructive pelvic surgery (highest level of training for pelvic floor disorders)
- Prolific researcher — 44 publications on Google Scholar across peer-reviewed journals
- Patient education philosophy — "doctor = teacher" (from Latin *docere*); empowers shared decision-making
- In-office pelvic floor PTs + regional PT network across NE WI and MI UP
- Wide geographic reach — serves 34 cities across WI and MI UP including telehealth
- Balanced practice — equal time in office consultations and surgery; not just a surgeon pushing surgery
- Personal website with deep educational content — 270+ pages of condition/treatment information competitors don't have

## Objections
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "I'm embarrassed to talk about this" | These are common medical conditions — you're not alone, and there's no judgment here |
| "Is surgery the only option?" | Many conditions improve with conservative treatments first (PT, pessary, lifestyle changes, medications) |
| "It's too far to drive" | Telehealth available; multiple regional locations; most patients visit infrequently after initial evaluation |

**Anti-persona:** Patients seeking cosmetic/aesthetic gynecology; patients outside the WI/MI UP service area

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Symptoms worsening, current doctor can't help or dismisses concerns, quality of life declining
**Pull:** Subspecialist expertise, patient education approach, no referral needed, regional accessibility
**Habit:** Inertia of living with symptoms, loyalty to current OB/GYN, embarrassment
**Anxiety:** Fear of surgery, embarrassment discussing symptoms, insurance coverage uncertainty, "will it actually help?"
